# Letter 2 — Engagement letter to third-party penetration tester (US, generic)

**Use this letter if:** You are hiring an external firm or individual to conduct the test.

**This letter complements** (and does not replace) the Rules of Engagement and any master services agreement (MSA) between the parties.

---

[Your letterhead]

[Date]

[Tester name]
[Tester firm]
[Tester address]

**Re: Penetration test engagement — [Application name]**

Dear [Tester contact name]:

This letter confirms our engagement of [Tester firm] ("you" or the "Tester") by [Engaging Party legal name] ("we," "us," or the "Client") to conduct a penetration test of [Application name] under the terms set out below and in the accompanying Rules of Engagement document dated [date] (the "ROE"), which is incorporated herein by reference.

**1. Authority to test**

The Client is the owner and operator of the application and is responsible for any personal information it processes. The application is hosted on the following provider(s): [list]. By this letter, we authorize the Tester and the named individuals on its team to conduct security testing against the targets listed in section 4 of the ROE, during the engagement window stated there, using the techniques permitted there.

This authorization is granted for the purpose of identifying security weaknesses in the application so we can remediate them. It is not authorization to access, modify, or test (a) any system operated by the hosting provider(s), (b) any other tenant of the hosting provider(s), or (c) any third-party service in the application's call path. The Tester acknowledges that activity outside this defined scope may constitute unauthorized access under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030) and applicable state computer-crime statutes, and may breach the hosting provider's Acceptable Use Policy.

**2. Provider compliance**

The Tester acknowledges receipt of the Provider Policy Reference (Document 04) summarizing the hosting provider's testing rules and confirms that:

- The Tester has reviewed the current Acceptable Use Policy and any testing-specific rules of the hosting provider(s);
- Where the hosting provider requires pre-notification or pre-authorization, the Client has confirmed in writing that this has been completed (a copy is filed with this engagement);
- The Tester will conduct no activity that would breach the hosting provider's policies, including activities prohibited under those policies that are not otherwise prohibited by the ROE.

**3. Standards of work**

You represent and warrant that:

- The engagement will be led by [Lead tester name], who holds [OSCP / OSCE / OSEE / GIAC GPEN / GIAC GWAPT / GIAC GXPN / CCSP / CISSP / equivalent] and has [N] years of relevant experience, including specific experience with [the hosting provider(s) in scope].
- All personnel conducting the engagement are suitably qualified, background-checked, and supervised.
- Work will be performed in accordance with [NIST SP 800-115, OWASP WSTG, OWASP Cloud Security Testing Guide, PTES, MITRE ATT&CK, or your stated methodology] and to a standard of skill and care reasonably expected of a competent professional in the field.
- You maintain in force, for the duration of the engagement, the following insurance:
  - Professional liability (errors and omissions): not less than $[amount] per claim / $[amount] aggregate;
  - Commercial general liability: not less than $[amount] per occurrence;
  - Cyber liability: not less than $[amount];
  - Workers' compensation as required by law.
- Certificates of insurance evidencing the above will be provided on execution and on request, naming the Client as additional insured where commercially reasonable.

**4. Compliance with platform terms and law**

You agree to comply with:

- The Rules of Engagement in full.
- The hosting provider's Acceptable Use Policy, security testing rules, and any provider-specific terms attached as Document 04.
- The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, 18 U.S.C. § 1030; the Digital Millennium Copyright Act, 17 U.S.C. § 1201; the Electronic Communications Privacy Act and Stored Communications Act, 18 U.S.C. §§ 2510 et seq. and 2701 et seq.; and applicable state computer-crime and privacy statutes.
- All federal and state laws and regulations applicable to the data, systems, and parties involved.

You will not subcontract any part of the engagement without our prior written consent.

**5. Data handling and regulated data**

In respect of any personal information, protected health information, cardholder data, or other regulated data you handle in connection with the engagement, you agree that:

- You will handle such data only as necessary to perform the services and on our documented instructions, which are set out in the ROE.
- Personnel with access to such data are subject to confidentiality obligations not less protective than those in this letter.
- You will implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards, including encryption of data at rest and in transit, access controls, and secure destruction of test artifacts.
- You will not store, process, or transmit such data outside the United States without our prior written consent.
- You will cooperate with us in responding to any data subject request, security incident, or regulatory inquiry that arises from the engagement.
- You will notify us without undue delay, and in no event more than 24 hours after discovery, of any actual or suspected unauthorized access to, acquisition of, or disclosure of such data.
- On completion of the engagement, you will return or destroy all such data and provide written confirmation of destruction.

If protected health information will be involved, a separate Business Associate Agreement will be executed and will prevail over this section to the extent of any inconsistency. If a separate Data Processing Agreement exists between the parties, it prevails over this section to the extent of any inconsistency.

**6. Reporting**

You will deliver the reports described in section 13 of the ROE within the times stated. You will not disclose any finding to any third party without our prior written consent, except where you are required to do so by law, subpoena, or a regulator with jurisdiction over you, in which case you will notify us first if not legally prohibited from doing so. Findings affecting the hosting provider's platform are not yours to disclose to the provider — the Client will handle any platform-affecting disclosures.

**7. Fees and expenses**

Fees for the engagement are $[amount], invoiced [terms — e.g., 50% on commencement, 50% on report delivery, net 30]. Reasonable expenses pre-approved in writing are reimbursable on receipt. Retest of remediated findings within [30] days of report delivery is included in the fee.

**8. Term and termination**

This engagement commences on [date] and continues until the deliverables in section 13 of the ROE are accepted. Either party may terminate immediately on written notice in the event of a material, uncured breach by the other. The Client may terminate without cause on [X] days' written notice; in that event, fees for work performed and non-cancellable commitments up to termination remain payable.

**9. Indemnification and liability**

The Tester will indemnify and defend the Client from third-party claims arising from (a) the Tester's breach of this letter, the ROE, or any hosting-provider Acceptable Use Policy, (b) the Tester's negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) the Tester's breach of confidentiality or data-handling obligations.

The Client will indemnify and defend the Tester from third-party claims arising from activities conducted within the scope and rules defined in the ROE, except to the extent such claims arise from matters for which the Tester indemnifies the Client.

The Tester's aggregate liability under this engagement is limited as set out in the MSA between the parties [or: limited to two times the fees paid under this engagement], except that this limitation does not apply to (i) breach of confidentiality, (ii) breach of data-handling obligations, (iii) indemnification obligations, (iv) breach of hosting-provider Acceptable Use Policy, or (v) willful misconduct or gross negligence.

**10. Independent contractor**

The Tester is an independent contractor. Nothing in this engagement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship.

**11. Governing law and venue**

This engagement is governed by the laws of the State of [Delaware / California / Engaging Party's state], without regard to conflict of laws principles. The parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in [County, State] for any dispute arising hereunder, and waive any objection to such venue.

**12. Entire agreement**

This letter, together with the ROE, the Provider Policy Reference, and the MSA (if any), constitutes the entire agreement of the parties with respect to the engagement and supersedes any prior or contemporaneous understanding on the subject.

**13. Confirmation**

Please countersign and return a copy of this letter, along with the Rules of Engagement, to confirm your acceptance.
